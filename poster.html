Stock Price Prediction: A Multi-Model Comparative Analysis

Zetao Huang huan2635@umn.edu

Problem Statement

The challenge of accurately predicting stock prices remains one of the most complex problems in financial markets due to:

  • High volatility and non-linear patterns
  • Multiple influencing factors
  • Temporal dependencies
  • Market inefficiencies

Background

Traditional methods often fall short in capturing complex market dynamics. Modern machine learning approaches offer promising alternatives by learning complex patterns from historical data.

Methods

Data Preprocessing

  • Historical stock data (2019-2022)
  • Technical indicators (MA5, MA20)
  • Economic indicators
  • Feature scaling

Random Forest

Model Architecture

  • Ensemble learning approach
  • GridSearchCV optimization
  • Feature importance analysis

Performance Metrics

  • RMSE: $8.47
  • MAE: $6.87
  • R² Score: 0.5190

Visualizations

Conclusion

Random Forest offers excellent computational efficiency and interpretability through feature importance analysis, though with moderate prediction accuracy compared to deep learning models.

LSTM Network

Model Architecture

  • Memory cells for long-term patterns
  • Dropout layers
  • Callbacks for checkpointing

Performance Metrics

  • RMSE: $0.12
  • MAE: $0.10
  • R² Score: -0.9487
  • Direction Accuracy: 48.41%

Visualizations

Conclusion

LSTM shows promising ability to capture temporal dependencies in stock data, but exhibits signs of overfitting despite low error metrics, requiring careful hyperparameter tuning.

Moirai Transformer

Model Architecture

  • Self-attention mechanism
  • Positional encoding
  • Cross-validation strategy

Performance Metrics

  • RMSE: $14.18
  • MAE: $11.22
  • R² Score: 0.8838
  • Direction Accuracy: 52.66%

Visualizations

Conclusion

Moirai Transformer demonstrates superior overall performance with the highest R² score and direction accuracy, leveraging self-attention mechanisms to effectively capture complex market patterns.
